About

I am an analyst with experience in financial and economic research, specializing in stress testing, macro-financial analysis, scenario modeling, and quantitative research involving large financial and macroeconomic datasets. My work has focused on understanding how macroeconomic and financial risks affect banks, financial systems, and the wider economy.

Over time, I have worked on financial risks, sovereign–bank linkages, climate risk, and the resilience of the financial system. Much of this work has involved building analytical frameworks, working with large and varied datasets, and translating technical findings into clear reports, presentations, and publications.

I am currently completing a Master of Management Studies (Systemic Risk track) at the Yale School of Management, and previously served nearly five years as a Senior Analyst at the Central Bank of Pakistan. I enjoy learning and working with advanced analytical tools for developing models that help us understand the world better.

Experience

Senior Analyst, Financial Stability Department

Central Bank of Pakistan (State Bank of Pakistan)

December 2020 – August 2025

Karachi, Pakistan

  • Executed system-wide capital stress tests across 52 financial institutions (USD 220B total assets) over a three-year projection horizon, covering credit risk, market risk (FX, equity, yield curve), liquidity risk (LCR/NSFR), and operational risk. Produced quarterly capital adequacy reports for the Financial Stability Committee, Deputy Governors, and Governors.
  • Developed a suite of Vector Auto-Regression (VAR) and regression-based econometric models projecting bank-level balance sheets, capital ratios, and risk-weighted assets under adverse macroeconomic scenarios, applying a dynamic balance sheet methodology that endogenously links loan growth and NPL formation to GDP, inflation, and exchange rate shocks — forming the central bank’s CCAR-equivalent stress testing toolkit.
  • Built duration-based interest rate risk models quantifying fixed-income portfolio sensitivity and net interest income (NII) impact under parallel, steepening, and flattening rate shock scenarios, with outputs directly informing Monetary Policy Committee and Financial Stability Committee deliberations on capital adequacy.
  • Assessed banking sector liquidity resilience using LCR and NSFR frameworks under acute and structural shock scenarios across 52 financial institutions, identifying funding vulnerabilities and informing supervisory action.
  • Reviewed and examined ICAAPs of major commercial banks against Basel III standards, issuing formal recommendations that contributed to improvements in stress testing frameworks and capital planning methodologies industry-wide.
  • Led Pakistan’s first climate risk stress testing program, applying NGFS scenario frameworks and building a carbon tax logit regression model on a panel of 343 listed corporates (2018–2023), estimating ECL impact of Rs 55 billion at $50/tCO2. Contributed to enabling approximately USD 1B IMF Resilience and Sustainability Facility access for Pakistan.
  • Monitored and synthesized global and domestic macro-financial conditions quarterly, tracking central bank policy cycles, inflation, interest rates, equity markets, commodity prices, and geopolitical risk spillovers. Authored the macro-financial developments chapter of the FSR (2021, 2022, 2024).
  • Built Power BI dashboards tracking macro-financial indicators for senior central bank leadership. Authored credit risk analysis covering USD 70B loan portfolio. Prepared policy briefs and presentations for FSB and IMF forums on behalf of senior central bank leadership.
