About

I am a financial analyst with experience in macro-financial research, quantitative modeling, and risk analysis. My work has focused on understanding how economic and financial developments shape banks, financial markets, and the broader economy.

Over the past several years, I have worked on stress testing, scenario analysis, climate risk, and financial stability assessment, using large financial and macroeconomic datasets to evaluate risk and inform decision-making. I enjoy combining rigorous quantitative analysis with practical insights to understand markets, identify emerging risks, and evaluate investment-relevant themes.

I recently graduated from the Yale School of Management with a Master of Management Studies (Systemic Risk Track) and previously spent nearly five years at the Central Bank of Pakistan.

Senior Analyst, Financial Stability Department

Central Bank of Pakistan (State Bank of Pakistan)

December 2020 – August 2025

Karachi, Pakistan

  • Designed and executed end-to-end macro stress tests of Pakistan’s entire banking sector covering 52 institutions with USD 220B in total assets, assessing credit risk (PD/LGD/EAD), interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB), market risk (equity and FX), and liquidity risk (LCR, NSFR, ALM mismatch), and delivering results to senior management, the Board Risk Committee, and the Financial Stability Committee on a quarterly basis.
  • Designed macro stress scenarios by combining VAR and DSGE model-based macro projections, historical shock calibration, and expert judgment, producing internally consistent multi-period paths for GDP, inflation, interest rates, FX depreciation, and equity prices across baseline, adverse, and severely adverse scenarios.
  • Developed econometric and financial models for projecting bank-level balance sheets, income statements, capital ratios, and risk metrics under baseline and adverse macro scenarios, enabling dynamic multi-year capital adequacy assessments across Pakistan’s banking sector and identifying institutions breaching regulatory minimums with quantified capital shortfalls for supervisory action.
  • Quantified physical climate risk across Pakistan’s banking sector using a multi-layer geospatial methodology, geocoding bank loan portfolio exposures against district and sector-level flood vulnerability maps and computing expected credit losses under historical shock scenarios by applying scenario-defined damage rates to institution-level exposures.
  • Modeled transition risk using NGFS-aligned carbon tax scenarios, tracing policy shocks through revenue compression and debt serviceability impairment for non-financial corporates across high-emission sectors, and translating the resulting PD escalation into increased expected credit losses and capital risk for exposed banks.
  • Key contributor to Pakistan’s first climate stress testing guidelines for the financial sector, serving as technical lead on the working group, with the guidelines submitted as a formal conditionality under Pakistan’s IMF Resilience and Sustainability Facility (RSF), directly linking the analytical work to Pakistan’s access to USD 1B in IMF climate-linked financing.
  • Compiled bank-level physical damage and NPL deterioration data following the 2022 Pakistan floods and coordinated directly with the World Bank PDNA team to integrate the financial sector damage assessment into the joint Post-Disaster Needs Assessment published by the World Bank, UN, EU, and ADB.
  • Developed econometric models to quantify how macro-financial shocks propagate through the banking sector, capturing the transmission of GDP contractions, inflation surges, interest rate cycles, and FX depreciations into bank-level asset quality, capital adequacy, profitability, and financial intermediation capacity across Pakistan’s financial system.
  • Researched Pakistan’s sovereign-bank nexus, analyzing the structural concentration of government securities on bank balance sheets, crowding-out effects on private sector credit intermediation, and amplification of sovereign risk through the banking system, producing leverage stress test models that contributed to balance sheet restructuring at Pakistan’s largest systemically important institutions.
  • Led a comprehensive financial viability assessment of Pakistan’s microfinance banking sector at the direction of the Deputy Governor, combining cross-country benchmarking, financial statement analysis of domestic institutions, and structured CFO interviews, producing recommendations that directly informed the revised prudential regulations released in 2025.
  • Authored the Global and Domestic Macroeconomic Developments chapter of the Financial Stability Review for three consecutive editions (2021, 2022, 2024), delivering a full macroeconomic diagnostic spanning global growth dynamics, inflation trends, trade and commodity developments, sovereign debt sustainability, and interest rate conditions, alongside a Pakistan-specific deep-dive covering GDP, CPI, external accounts, monetary conditions, and fiscal position, supported by original data analysis, trend modeling, and publication-quality visualizations translating complex macro-financial developments into authoritative policy narrative for domestic and international audiences.
  • Built and maintained automated data pipelines in Python (pandas, requests, openpyxl, sqlalchemy), R (tidyverse, httr, rvest), and VBA to collect, clean, and consolidate banking, macroeconomic, and financial market datasets feeding the recurring stress testing model iterations.
  • Designed the stress testing supervisory data submission templates from scratch with built-in validation rules, dropdown controls, and cross-check formulas, and owned the full data lifecycle across 52 institutions from submission coordination through validation, reconciliation, and analytical readiness each quarterly cycle.
  • Developed Python scripts to automate production of publication-quality charts, tables, and dashboards for stress testing reports and FSR chapters using matplotlib, seaborn, and plotly for visualization and python-docx for programmatic report assembly, replacing manual workflows and ensuring reproducibility across quarterly reporting cycles.
  • Authored chapters for the Financial Stability Review, the Central Bank of Pakistan’s statutory publication mandated by law and submitted to Parliament and Senate, covering banking sector resilience and stress testing (2022, 2023, 2024), climate risk (2022, 2023, 2024), and global macro-financial developments (2021, 2022, 2024), with each edition passing through rigorous Director, Deputy Governor, Governor, and Board review before submission.
  • Produced the quarterly credit risk report covering USD 70B in banking sector credit exposure, analyzing NPL formation and migration trends, provision coverage adequacy relative to estimated expected losses, sector-level and borrower-level concentration risk, and implied probabilities of default for Pakistan’s largest systemic borrowers informing credit risk views at the macro level.
  • Prepared policy memos and speeches on climate risk and financial stability for senior management’s high-level engagements including with the IMF, World Bank, and Financial Stability Board.
  • Conducted independent validation of stress testing and macroeconomic forecasting models across Central Bank departments, covering conceptual soundness, data quality and input integrity, parameter calibration, output reasonableness, and documentation completeness, performing the full scope of a model risk management function in a supervisory context.
  • Reviewed stress testing model submissions from major commercial banks (ICAAP), challenging macro scenario design, credit loss model methodology, assumptions, and capital planning narrative, and issued formal written findings driving framework improvements at Pakistan’s systemically important institutions.
  • Built benchmarking and backtesting frameworks to assess the ongoing predictive performance of the stress testing model suite, constructing challenger models using alternative functional forms, comparing outputs to identify systematic biases, and recalibrating satellite model parameters based on realized outcomes.
  • Served as the Central Bank of Pakistan’s working-level counterpart across three concurrent multilateral engagements with the World Bank, UN, EU, ADB, FCDO, and IMF on post-disaster financial sector assessment, climate stress testing methodology, and Pakistan’s USD 1B IMF Resilience and Sustainability Facility program.
  • Participated in a cross-regulator working group with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan to develop climate disclosure requirements and a green taxonomy for financial institutions, drawing on TCFD, ISSB (IFRS S1/S2), and EU Taxonomy standards.
